      Water Turbidity

      Water turbidity is the causation of suspended and dissolved particles in water bodies that scatter light making the water appear cloudy or murky.

       

      What particles are commonly found in turbid waters?

       

      Particulated matter includes:

      • Sediment

      • Clay

      • Silt

      • Fine organic and inorganic matter

      • Soluble colored organic compounds

      • algae and other microscopic organisms

      Green Roofs

      Green roofs, also known as vegetative or eco-roofs, are a layer of vegetation planted over a waterproofing system that is installed over a flat or slightly-slanted roof.

       

      There are three main types of green roofs:

      • Extensive - A shallow growing medium of less than 6 inches with a modest roof load

      • Intensive - More soil and deeper growing medium ranging from several feet that can support diverse selections and small trees

      • Semi-Intensive - Includes features of both extensive and intensive green roofs.

      The depths of green roofs may depend on the roof structure, plant, annual rainfall, and stormwater performance requirements.
